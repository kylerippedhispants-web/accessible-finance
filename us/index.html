Tax basics

Your paycheck has more than one layer

U.S. taxes are not one single rate. Federal income tax, state rules, and payroll taxes can each affect what reaches your bank account.

Educational overview only. Tax rules and personal outcomes vary, so confirm decisions with current IRS and state guidance.
01

Federal income tax

A progressive system where different portions of taxable income can fall into different brackets.

02

State and local tax

Your location matters. Some states have no individual income tax, while others use flat or progressive rates.

03

Payroll tax

Social Security and Medicare taxes are separate from ordinary federal income tax withholding.

A sensible order

Build the foundation before optimizing everything

Financial progress is usually a sequence, not a perfect spreadsheet completed all at once.

01

Stabilize the basics

Know your cash flow, build an emergency reserve, and make a plan for high-cost debt.

02

Capture available benefits

Understand your workplace plan, especially any employer match and the investment choices inside it.

03

Invest with a purpose

Match the account, asset mix, and time horizon to the goal the money is meant to support.

Financial independence

FIRE is about options, not one retirement age

Financial Independence, Retire Early focuses on widening the gap between income and spending, investing that gap consistently, and building enough flexibility to choose how you spend your time.

01

Know your annual spending

Your spending level shapes how much invested wealth may be needed to support it.

02

Raise the savings rate sustainably

A durable plan leaves room for the life you are living now, not only the future version.

03

Plan across account types

Retirement accounts and flexible taxable investments can play different roles before and after traditional retirement age.
